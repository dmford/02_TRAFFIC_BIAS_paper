\section{Delineating High- vs Low-Scrutiny Events}

Using a k-density plot, I separate events into high- vs low-scrutiny, by taking the mean deceased search level before and after their death. A large difference here implies a highly-scrutinized death-event. With a mass above 0, this resulted in a threshold of 0.2\% relative to Michael Brown's peak. \\

To give some perspective, my Brown was searched in the US: 81\% as much as actor Alan Rickman, 97\% as much author as Maya Angelou, 123\% as much as the gorilla Harambe, and 185\% as much as actor Leonard Nimoy. \\
