After noting some anomalous pretrends between T-7/6/5 and T-4/3/2, I investigated further to determine what was happening in the data. I noticed first that some large statewide-department and highly-scrutinized event was showing abnormally-low numbers of stops in bins T-4/3/2 relative to T-7/6/5 or T-1. Shown below are the mean numbers of stops for those three timeframes, where I have isolated the Florida Highway Patrol, a rather large department as the likely culprit, although so far I have not determined the underlying reason. \\

Continuing as above, I looked into non-statewide low-scrutiny events and found three events that are driving an unusually-low number of stops in periods T-7/6/5. First off is Aurora Police in Colorado, who appear to have unusually-low stop numbers starting on Christmas Eve and continuing until New Years, suggesting that the period between the two holidays may need to be included in my ``Holidays'' dummy variable. \\
